# Evoke Privacy Policy

**Last updated: May 27, 2026**

Evoke helps you stay connected with the people who matter. This policy explains what data Evoke handles, how it's stored, and your rights.

**The short version:** your relationship data — contacts, check-ins, notes, and preferences — is private to you. It lives on your device and in your personal iCloud account, syncing across your Apple devices. We have no servers that hold it, can't read it, and never share or sell it. Evoke collects only anonymous usage and diagnostic data to fix bugs and improve the app — never your contacts, names, or messages — and you can turn that off in Settings.

---

## What data does Evoke store?

- **Contact information** you add: names, phone numbers, email addresses, relationship labels, and notes
- **Connection history**: dates and methods of your check-ins
- **Reminders**: notification times and frequencies you set
- **Preferences**: display name, notification and appearance settings, onboarding choices
- **Contact photos**: thumbnails you import for contacts you add

## Where is my data stored?

Your relationship data lives in two places, both private to you: **locally on your device** (using Apple's SwiftData) and **in your personal iCloud account** (Apple's CloudKit private database), so it stays in sync across your Apple devices.

Evoke has **no servers or storage of its own**. The CloudKit private database is part of Apple's infrastructure under your Apple ID — **the developer has no access to your synced data.** We can't read it, query it, or share it. If you turn off iCloud Sync in Settings, your data simply stays on a single device.

## Does Evoke collect analytics or diagnostics?

Yes — but only **anonymous** data, kept separate from your relationship data, and you can turn it off.

- **Usage analytics**: anonymous product-interaction events (such as which screens are viewed and where key flows start and finish). These carry only non-identifying counts and labels — **never your name, your contacts, your messages, notes, or photos.**
- **Crash & diagnostics**: crash reports and performance metrics so we can find and fix problems.

This data is processed by **third-party analytics and diagnostics providers** acting on our behalf, under agreements that require them to protect it and use it only to provide their service to us. They receive only anonymous data tied to a random identifier — never your identity. We do **not** record your screen, show ads, track you across other apps or websites, work with data brokers, or sell your data.

You can turn this off anytime in **Settings → "Diagnostic Analytics."** When you do, Evoke stops sending events and clears any buffered on your device.

## Does Evoke access my device contacts?

Only if you choose to import them, with your permission via the iOS prompt. Imported contacts are copied into Evoke's local database (and your iCloud, if Sync is on) as one-time snapshots — Evoke doesn't continuously sync with your iOS Contacts app or send your contacts to any developer-controlled server. You can delete them from Evoke anytime without affecting your iOS Contacts. If you deny access, Evoke works fine — add contacts manually.

## Does Evoke send notifications?

Only if you enable them. Reminders are generated entirely on your device as iOS local notifications — no external push service. They're customizable and can be disabled anytime.

## Your rights and control

- **View / edit**: all your data is visible and editable in the app
- **Export**: download a complete copy of your data as a JSON file from Settings (generated locally; we keep no copy)
- **Delete**: "Delete All Data" in Settings erases everything on the device immediately; if iCloud Sync is on, the deletion propagates to your iCloud and your other devices
- **Opt out**: turn off "Diagnostic Analytics" in Settings to stop all anonymous usage and diagnostic collection

Because your data lives only on your device and in your personal iCloud, there's no Evoke-controlled account to delete and no server request needed.

## Children's privacy

Evoke is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect their information. A parent or guardian can erase all data using "Delete All Data" in Settings.

## Changes & contact

If this policy changes, the updated version will be available in the app (Settings → Privacy Policy) and at this URL, with a new "Last updated" date. If you have questions about this policy or your data, you can reach us through the Evoke app (Settings → Privacy → Privacy Questions).
